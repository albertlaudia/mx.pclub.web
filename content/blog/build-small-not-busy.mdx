I've been building apps for 8 years. I shipped my first one in 2018. I sold a company in 2022. I'm building four apps right now.

The advice I'd give my 2018 self: **build small, not busy.**

## What "busy" looks like

Busy is:
- Shipping 12 features last month
- Tracking 47 metrics
- Three Slack channels, two standups, four Asana boards
- Three apps in the App Store, all updated monthly
- A "platform" with documentation
- A growth team
- A/B tests running on every screen
- "We're exploring 8 different verticals"

Busy feels productive. It's almost never what produces the result.

## What "small" looks like

Small is:
- 1 thing shipped last month, and it's good
- 1 metric that matters (and 2 you can ignore)
- 1 Slack channel
- 1 app, working
- A 4-page Notion doc instead of a wiki
- 1 person + 1 designer
- A landing page that explains what the app does in 50 words
- Saying "no" to 19 things this quarter

Small doesn't feel productive. It feels *lazy*. That's how you know you're doing it right.

## The math of small

The dirty secret: most of what you ship doesn't matter. 80% of your features are used by 20% of your users, and most of those 20% would be fine without them.

If you ship 4 features this quarter and only 1 matters, you have 1 win and 3 sunk costs.
If you ship 1 feature this quarter and it matters, you have 1 win and 0 sunk costs.

**The small team moves faster because the small team is faster, not because they work harder.**

## The rise of "small"

The pclub ecosystem is the bet I'm making on this:

- **1perc** does one thing (5-minute book summaries)
- **HEAL** does one thing (3-minute audio resets)
- **Riseup** does one thing (morning ritual prompts)
- **Resonate** does one thing (tune a guitar)

None of them measure, notify, gamify, or optimize for engagement. None of them are "platforms." None of them are "exploring 8 different verticals."

They're small. They each have a homepage that fits in 800 pixels. Each one is something I want to use myself.

## The hardest part

The hardest part isn't building small. It's *staying* small. The temptation to add "just one more feature" never goes away. The investor emails asking "what's your moat" never stop. The competitor shipping 6 things makes you feel like you should be shipping 7.

But here's what I keep telling myself: **the apps that win the next decade will be the ones that do the fewest things**. The cost of attention is now higher than the cost of code. The apps that respect that win.

The apps that respect that look like ours. Small. Calm. One thing, then disappear.

## Build small

If you're building something right now, look at your roadmap. If you have more than 3 things, cut it to 3. Then cut it to 2. Then ship one.

That's how Riseup started. That's how Resonate shipped. That's how 1perc stayed focused on books instead of "personal development."

Small is hard. Small ships. Build small.
